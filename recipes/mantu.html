Mantu

Description

Mantu is a beloved Afghan dish that encapsulates the rich culinary heritage of Afghanistan. These delectable dumplings are typically stuffed with a savory mixture of spiced ground meat, often beef or lamb, and aromatic onions. The dumplings are then carefully folded into delicate pockets and steamed to perfection. Served atop a bed of yogurt and garnished with a tantalizing tomato-based sauce, along with a sprinkle of dried mint, Mantu is a flavorful masterpiece. Its delightful blend of textures and flavors, coupled with its cultural significance, makes Mantu a true Afghan delicacy, enjoyed with family and friends during special occasions and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Ground Meat
  • Onions
  • Spices
  • Dough
  • Yogurt
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Mint

Instructions

  1. Prepare the meat filling by combining ground meat, onions, and spices.
  2. Create a dough with flour, water, and a pinch of salt, then let it rest.
  3. Roll out the dough and form thin circles or squares.
  4. Place a spoonful of filling in each dough piece and fold to create dumplings.
  5. Steam the dumplings for about 30-40 minutes until cooked.
  6. Mix yogurt with garlic for a flavorful sauce.
  7. Prepare a tomato-based sauce with tomatoes and garlic.
  8. Serve hot, topped with yogurt sauce and tomato sauce, garnished with dried mint.
